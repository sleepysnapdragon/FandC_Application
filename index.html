ABOUT ME

On 17th December 1989 the very first episode of The Simpsons was broadcast. And a lesser-known fact is that over on the other side of the Atlantic, I was born. A fairly uneventful English childhood ensued, with companions Teddy the teddy bear and Penguin the guinea pig.

I then migrated north of the border to study English Literature, Language and Latin at the University of Edinburgh. After university, in the absence of better ideas, I went to work in the London insurance market, where I learned about risk and financial modelling.

4 years later I faced up to the fact that the insurance industry wasn't really my thing, and turned down offers of an extensive sabbatical to quit outright and seek new pastures.

I had always wanted to live in a non-English speaking country and learn a new language, and in light of the Brexit referendum result, 2017 seemed like a good time to do that.

Off I went to Italy, Rome to be precise, with some suncream and a minor Duolingo-induced thumb sprain. Here I've spent two years teaching English (some English curiosity)

WHY I'M APPLYING

I've been interested in coding for a long time but it's only recently that I've really believed that this could be my career. A long, long, time ago... around Y2K... I taught myself HTML with an online tutorial. I enjoyed that and I made a couple of little websites for myself but I never knew where to go afterwards. When I started work I did a range of tasks: administrative, analytical, communications... But I always found that I enjoyed the logical problem-solving the most. I became an expert at stretching Microsoft Excel to within an inch of its life. After leaving that job, and while I've been teaching English, I've also been slowly learning to code. Founders and Coders appeals to me because of its peer-led structure, which I think encourages whilst encouraging individuals' hard-work and ownership of their learning: you're not paying someone to tell you the answer.
